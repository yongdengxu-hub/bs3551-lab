Forecasts & prediction intervals

Watch the point forecast converge and the interval fan out. This is exam question 2.

From the end of the sample, an AR forecast does two things at once: the point forecast decays geometrically toward the unconditional mean μ = c / (1 − φ), and the forecast error grows, so the 95% interval fans out and settles at the long-run width. Set the model, read the 1-step interval off the table, then push the horizon out and see why long-horizon forecasts are "just the mean, with wide bands".

How to use it in class

Give the students the AR(1) numbers (c, φ, σ, yT) and ask them to compute the 1-step forecast and its 95% interval by hand, then reveal the table to check. Slide φ toward 1 to show how persistence widens the long-run band; slide the horizon out to show the point forecast collapsing onto μ.
