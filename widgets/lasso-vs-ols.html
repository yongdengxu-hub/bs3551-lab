LASSO vs OLS

Why a penalty beats least squares when you have too many predictors. Exam question 5.

← back to lab

There are 12 candidate predictors (think of them as candidate lags), but only three actually drive the outcome. OLS fits all of them and chases the noise; a penalty pulls the useless coefficients back. LASSO (an L1 penalty) sets them to exactly zero, so it selects a model. Ridge (L2) shrinks everything but keeps it. Slide the penalty λ and watch the coefficients, and the train-vs-test gap, respond.

How to use it in class

Start on LASSO with the penalty in the middle: only the three real predictors survive (selection). Slide the penalty all the way right and watch every coefficient light up while the test error climbs above the train error, that is overfitting. Switch to Ridge: the test error still improves, but no coefficient is ever exactly zero, so Ridge shrinks but does not select. This is the whole of exam question 5 in two minutes.
